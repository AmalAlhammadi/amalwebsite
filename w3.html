3D printing and sacnning

 

The production of a three-dimensional object from a CAD or digital 3D model is known as 3D printing or additive manufacturing. 3D printing can apply to various methods in which material is deposited, connected, or solidified under computer control to make a three-dimensional object, with material added layer by layer (such as polymers, liquids, or powder grains)

 

After learning how to use a laser cutter to design such beautiful products, 3D printing was also discussed to complete our thinking design and be able to create our final project.Ultimaker Cura software was used during the lab session to design the tested element. Each of us was allocated to design a different test, even with support material or not. My chosen one was an unsupported infill type of PLA material

 

  Group assignment

Below are the test steps:

1-Download Ultimaker Cura Software

2-Open your test file ended with (.stl extension)

 

3-Select the printer you are working with (Ultimaker 3 extended)

 

4-Adjust the model's setting by clicking on the marketplace at the top right of the page

 

The setting has been adjusted based on the material and the used printer. It takes for me 1:26 h to complete printing my small prototype. Learning how to operate the machine and how to get used to it was a challenging, significant part, especially when changing the used material and loaded it

 

5-click on slice, then preview >> it will show you the printed time

 

6-Save to disk >> as a gcode file

 

7-Go to the printer >> material 1 >> change

since its an unsupported test, we will change only the 1st material

8-Wait until the previous material totally removed

9-Place the new material in the holder

10-Click load material >> choose its type (PLA)

11-Click confirm while starting extruding

 

Congratulation, your model is printed!!
